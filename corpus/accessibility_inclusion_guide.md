## Language and Terminology

### Inclusive Language

**We use:**
- Person-first language (when appropriate)
- Identity-first language (when preferred)
- Gender-neutral terms (when applicable)
- Respectful terminology
- Age-appropriate language

**Examples:**
- "Child who uses a wheelchair" or "wheelchair user" (based on preference)
- "Autistic child" or "child with autism" (based on preference)
- "Parent" instead of assuming "mom" or "dad"
- "Grown-up" or "caregiver" when appropriate

### Avoiding Assumptions

**We don't assume:**
- Gender based on appearance
- Family structure
- Cultural background
- Abilities
- Preferences

**We ask:**
- Preferred pronouns
- Family configuration
- Cultural elements to include
- Specific needs
- Representation preferences

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can you represent my child's specific disability?**
A: Yes! Contact us to discuss. We'll work with you to ensure accurate, respectful representation.

**Q: Do you charge extra for diverse representation?**
A: No! Basic representation (skin tone, hair, basic features) is included. Only complex customizations have additional fees.

**Q: Can you include our two moms/two dads?**
A: Absolutely! Add family members during customization and specify relationships.

**Q: My child uses they/them pronouns. Can you do that?**
A: Yes! Select they/them pronouns during customization.

**Q: Can you include our cultural traditions?**
A: Yes! Cultural customization is available for $12. Provide details in special instructions.

**Q: What if I'm not sure how to request something?**
A: Contact us! We're happy to discuss and help you create the perfect representation.
