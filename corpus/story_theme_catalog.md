# Story Theme Catalog

## Available Story Themes

We offer 7 core story themes designed to help children navigate important life moments. Each theme is customizable and age-appropriate.

---

## 1. Potty Training Adventures

**Recommended Ages:** 2-4 years

### Story Overview
Join your child on an exciting journey to becoming a "big kid" who uses the potty! This encouraging story celebrates each step of the potty training process with positive reinforcement and relatable scenarios.

### Learning Objectives
- Understanding body signals
- Building confidence in bathroom independence
- Establishing routine
- Celebrating successes
- Handling accidents positively

### Story Variations

| Variation | Focus | Best For |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| Daytime Training | Using potty during the day | Beginning potty training |
| Nighttime Training | Staying dry at night | After daytime success |
| Public Restrooms | Using bathrooms away from home | Confident daytime users |
| Transition from Diapers | Saying goodbye to diapers | Motivation to start |

### Customizable Elements
- Type of potty (standalone, seat, etc.)
- Rewards system your family uses
- Specific challenges (fear of flushing, etc.)
- Favorite bathroom books or toys
- Sibling who's already trained

### Key Story Moments
1. Recognizing the "potty feeling"
2. First successful potty use
3. Washing hands routine
4. Handling an accident with grace
5. Celebration and reward
6. Becoming a "big kid"

### Sample Pages
- Page count: 16-20 pages
- Illustrations: Colorful, encouraging bathroom scenes
- Tone: Positive, celebratory, patient

## 2. First Day at School

**Recommended Ages:** 3-6 years

### Story Overview
Experience the excitement and nervousness of starting school through your child's eyes. This story helps ease anxiety by showing what to expect and emphasizing the fun of learning and making friends.

### Learning Objectives
- Reducing separation anxiety
- Understanding school routines
- Building social confidence
- Excitement about learning
- Coping with new environments

### Story Variations

| Variation | Focus | Best For |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| First Day Ever | Very first school experience | Preschool starters |
| New School | Changing schools | Moving or transitioning |
| Kindergarten | Starting "big kid" school | 5-6 year olds |
| Making Friends | Social aspects emphasized | Shy children |

### Customizable Elements
- Type of school (preschool, kindergarten, elementary)
- School name (generic or specific)
- Teacher's name and gender
- Classroom activities
- Best friend or sibling at school
- Specific anxieties to address

### Key Story Moments
1. Getting ready in the morning
2. Saying goodbye to parent
3. Meeting the teacher
4. Exploring the classroom
5. Making a new friend
6. Fun learning activities
7. Pickup time reunion

### Sample Pages
- Page count: 20-24 pages
- Illustrations: Bright classroom scenes, diverse children
- Tone: Reassuring, exciting, warm

## 3. Flying High (First Flight)

**Recommended Ages:** 4-8 years

### Story Overview
Take off on an adventure as your child experiences their first airplane journey! This story demystifies air travel, from airport security to landing, making flying fun rather than frightening.

### Learning Objectives
- Understanding airport procedures
- Reducing fear of flying
- Managing ear pressure
- Staying calm during turbulence
- Excitement about travel

### Story Variations

| Variation | Focus | Best For |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| Short Flight | Domestic, 1-3 hours | First-time flyers |
| Long Flight | International, 5+ hours | Building stamina |
| Airport Focus | Security, boarding process | Airport anxiety |
| In-Flight Focus | Takeoff, landing, turbulence | Flying fears |

### Customizable Elements
- Destination (beach, grandparents, Disney, etc.)
- Travel companions (parents, siblings, grandparents)
- Specific concerns (takeoff, landing, turbulence, bathrooms)
- Airline details (if desired)
- Activities during flight

### Key Story Moments
1. Packing and preparing
2. Arriving at the airport
3. Going through security
4. Waiting at the gate
5. Boarding the plane
6. Takeoff excitement
7. In-flight activities
8. Landing and arrival

### Sample Pages
- Page count: 24-28 pages
- Illustrations: Detailed airport and airplane scenes
- Tone: Adventurous, informative, exciting

## 4. Doctor Visit Heroes

**Recommended Ages:** 3-7 years

### Story Overview
Become a brave hero at the doctor's office! This story shows that doctor visits help us stay healthy and strong, making medical appointments less scary and more empowering.

### Learning Objectives
- Reducing medical anxiety
- Understanding doctor's role
- Cooperating with examinations
- Expressing feelings about discomfort
- Pride in being brave

### Story Variations

| Variation | Focus | Best For |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| Well-Child Checkup | Routine examination | Annual visits |
| Vaccination Visit | Getting shots | Immunization appointments |
| Sick Visit | When feeling unwell | Illness-related visits |
| Specific Procedure | Blood test, X-ray, etc. | Particular medical needs |

### Customizable Elements
- Type of visit (checkup, shots, specific procedure)
- Doctor's gender and name
- Specific medical tools (stethoscope, thermometer, etc.)
- Particular fears to address
- Reward after visit
- Parent or caregiver present

### Key Story Moments
1. Arriving at doctor's office
2. Waiting room activities
3. Meeting the doctor/nurse
4. Height and weight measurement
5. Examination process
6. Brave moment (shot or procedure)
7. Reward and celebration
8. Feeling proud

### Sample Pages
- Page count: 18-22 pages
- Illustrations: Friendly medical office, kind healthcare providers
- Tone: Reassuring, empowering, gentle

### Learning Objectives
- Adjusting to family changes
- Understanding baby needs
- Managing jealousy feelings
- Taking pride in being "big"
- Bonding with new sibling

### Story Variations

| Variation | Focus | Best For |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| Preparing for Baby | Before birth | Pregnancy period |
| Baby Arrives | First days home | Just after birth |
| Adjusting to Changes | New routines | First few months |
| Being a Helper | Big sibling role | Building confidence |

### Customizable Elements
- Baby's name (if born) or "baby brother/sister"
- Specific concerns (less attention, noise, etc.)
- New responsibilities
- Special big sibling privileges
- Family dynamics (single parent, two parents, etc.)
- Other siblings

### Key Story Moments
1. Learning about the baby
2. Preparing baby's room
3. Baby comes home
4. Holding the baby
5. Helping with baby care
6. Special time with parents
7. Proud big sibling moment

### Sample Pages
- Page count: 18-22 pages
- Illustrations: Warm family scenes, cute baby
- Tone: Loving, inclusive, reassuring

- Understanding sleep importance
- Self-soothing techniques

### Story Variations

| Variation | Focus | Best For |

| Overcoming Resistance | Bedtime battles | Resistant sleepers |

- Tone: Soothing, gentle, peaceful

### Learning Objectives
- Understanding sharing concepts
- Taking turns
- Managing disappointment
- Empathy for others
- Conflict resolution
- Making and keeping friends

### Story Variations

| Variation | Focus | Best For |
|-----------|-------|----------|

| Taking Turns | Patience and fairness | Preschoolers |
| Making Friends | Social initiation | Shy children |
| Resolving Conflicts | Problem-solving | Older children (5-6) |

### Customizable Elements

- Friend's name and appearance
- Types of toys or activities
- Social situations (playground, playdate, etc.)
- Sibling sharing scenarios
- Cultural aspects of friendship

### Key Story Moments
1. Playing alone happily
2. Friend wants to join
3. Initial reluctance to share
4. Trying sharing
5. Discovering sharing is fun
6. Taking turns
7. Conflict and resolution

### Sample Pages

- Illustrations: Playful scenes, diverse children, toys
- Tone: Encouraging, positive, relatable
